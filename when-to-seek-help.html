When to Seek Professional Help

While this toolkit is designed to help you self-manage common, uncomplicated deltoid ligament injuries, it does not replace professional medical advice. You should see a doctor or a physiotherapist if you experience any of the following "red flag" symptoms:

  • Inability to Bear Weight: You cannot take even 4 steps on the injured leg immediately after the injury or in the emergency room (this can be a sign of a fracture according to the Ottawa Ankle Rules).
  • Severe, Worsening Pain: Pain that is excruciating, throbbing, or getting progressively worse despite rest, ice, and elevation.
  • Gross Deformity: The ankle or foot looks visibly out of place or deformed.
  • Severe Instability: The ankle constantly gives way entirely, making you fall when you try to walk.
  • Numbness or Tingling: Experiencing pins and needles, loss of sensation, or paleness/coldness in the foot and toes.
  • No Improvement: You have been following the Phase 1 and 2 guidelines for 3-4 weeks but have noticed absolute zero improvement in your symptoms or function.
Who to see? A Physical Therapist (Physiotherapist) is the ideal professional for rehabilitation guidance. If you suspect a fracture or require medication, see a General Practitioner (GP) or visit an Urgent Care / Emergency Department.
