Common Symptoms

Knowing what to expect can help reduce anxiety. Here are the typical signs that indicate a deltoid ligament injury.

🔍 Pain on the Inside

The most noticeable symptom is deep aching or sharp pain directly on the inside of the ankle, right below the inner bony bump.

🎈 Swelling & Bruising

Puffiness on the inside of the ankle that can sometimes spread down into the foot. Bruising may appear a day or two after the injury.

📍 Tenderness to Touch

The ligament will feel extremely sensitive if you press on it, even lightly.

🦶 Pain Taking Weight

Standing, walking, or going down stairs may cause sharp twinges of pain as the ankle struggles to support your body weight.

⚠️ Feelings of Instability

A sensation that the ankle is "weak," wobbly, or feels like it might suddenly give way underneath you, especially on uneven ground.

Medical professionals grade sprains from 1 to 3 based on severity:

  • Grade 1 (Mild): Slight stretching of the ligament fibers. Mild tenderness and minor swelling. You can usually walk with some tightness.
  • Grade 2 (Moderate): Partial tear of the ligament. Noticeable swelling and pain. Walking is difficult and painful. The ankle feels slightly loose.
  • Grade 3 (Severe): Complete tear. Significant pain, swelling, and often severe bruising. Walking is almost impossible without support, and the joint feels very unstable.
