The 4-Phase Recovery Program

Progress through these phases at your own pace. There is no strict timeline—only move to the next phase when your ankle meets the progression signs.

Goals: Reduce inflammation and protect the healing ligament while maintaining gentle movement.

Exercises:

  • Ankle Pumps: Sitting or lying down, slowly point your toes away from you, then pull them back towards you. (15-20 reps, 3x a day)
  • Alphabet: Imagine your big toe is a pen and slowly draw the alphabet in the air. Keep the movement small to avoid sharp pain. (1-2 times a day)
  • Light Activation: Sit with your foot flat against a wall or immovable object. Gently push the outside of your foot into the wall for 5 seconds without letting the foot move. (5 reps, 2x a day)
Ready to Progress When: You can walk short distances around the house with minimal pain, and the initial sharp pain and major swelling have significantly reduced.

Goals: Strengthen the muscles supporting the ankle (especially the calves and inner ankle muscles) and restore full mobility.

Exercises:

  • Seated Calf Raises: Sit with feet flat on the floor. Push through your toes to lift your heels up high. Lower slowly. (3 sets of 15 reps, 1x a day)
  • Resistance Band Inversion: Secure a band around your foot. Gently pull the foot inward against the band's resistance. Return slowly. (3 sets of 10 reps, 1x a day)
  • Towel Stretch: Sit with legs straight. Loop a towel under your toes and gently pull towards you to stretch the calf. (Hold 30 seconds, 3x a day)
Ready to Progress When: You can perform these exercises easily, walk normally without a limp, and stand on the injured leg for 10 seconds without severe pain.

Goals: Train your nervous system to trust the ankle again (neuromuscular control) and improve balance.

Exercises:

  • Single Leg Balance: Stand near a wall for support. Lift your good leg and balance on your injured leg. Try to let go of the wall. (Hold 30-45 seconds, 3 reps)
  • Uneven Balance: Once single leg balance is easy, do it while standing on a folded towel or sofa cushion. (Hold 30 seconds, 3 reps)
  • Standing Calf Raises: Stand holding a wall. Push up onto your tiptoes with both feet, then slowly lower. (3 sets of 12 reps)
Ready to Progress When: You can balance on the injured leg on a cushion for 30 seconds without falling, and have no pain during daily activities.

Goals: Prepare the ankle for higher impact tasks like rapid walking, sports, or heavy daily activities.

Exercises:

  • Small Hops: Hop forward and backward over an imaginary line on two feet. Progress to side-to-side hops. (3 sets of 20 seconds)
  • Single Leg Calf Raises: Stand on just the injured leg. Push up onto your tiptoes and slowly lower. (3 sets of 8-10 reps)
  • Brisk Walking to Light Jogging: Start introducing small intervals of light jogging during your walks, gradually increasing the jogging time if pain-free.
Ready for Full Return When: You have full movement, full strength compared to the other leg, zero hesitation when hopping, and feel confident returning to your normal activities.
