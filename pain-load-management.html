Pain and Load Management

Complete rest is rarely the best answer for ankle injuries. Instead, the goal is Optimal Loading—finding the "sweet spot" of activity that stimulates healing without overloading the fragile new tissue.

Strategies for Daily Life

Pacing Your Day

If your job requires standing, try to break it up. Stand for 30 minutes, then sit for 10. Breaking tasks into smaller chunks prevents swelling from building up during the day.

Footwear Matters

Avoid completely flat shoes or high heels right now. A supportive sneaker with a slight heel drop and good arch support takes pressure off the inside of the ankle.

Modifying Activities

If a 30-minute walk causes pain, try two 15-minute walks instead. If stairs hurt, take them one step at a time (leading with the good leg going up, and the bad leg going down).

The 24-Hour Rule: If you try a new activity or exercise and your pain/swelling is significantly worse 24 hours later, you did slightly too much. Scale back the intensity by 20% next time.
