Why Women May Be More at Risk

Research shows that women, particularly between the ages of 18 and 40, often experience different rates and types of lower body injuries compared to men. Understanding these factors can help you prevent future injuries.

Joint Flexibility

Women naturally tend to have greater joint laxity (flexibility) due to hormonal differences. While great for mobility, it means the muscles have to work harder to stabilize the joint when the ligaments are naturally looser.

Muscle Activation

Studies show differences in how women activate their leg and hip muscles during landing or pivoting. Sometimes, this can lead to the knee dropping inward, which places direct stress on the inside of the ankle (the deltoid ligament).

Footwear Factors

Daily footwear choices, including narrow toe boxes, lack of arch support, or elevated heels, can alter the natural mechanics of the foot and reduce ankle stability over time.

Movement Demands

Many women balance multiple active roles—chasing toddlers, working on their feet, and participating in dynamic sports like netball or tennis, which heavily rely on sudden stops and changes of direction.

The Good News: While you can't change your joint flexibility, you can change your muscle strength and movement patterns. The Phase 2 and 3 exercises in this toolkit are specifically designed to address these risk factors!
